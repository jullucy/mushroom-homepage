About Mushrooms

Mushrooms are extraordinary organisms that play a crucial role in ecosystems. They are neither plants nor animals but form their own kingdom.

In the fall, as temperatures begin to drop, pumpkins and squash take all the glory, but let's not forget about edible mushrooms, the ever-versatile bumper crop! While mushrooms can be found year-round, many varieties peak during the fall and winter. And with the average American consuming more than two pounds of edible mushrooms each year, we wanted to take a closer look at the top varieties.

How to Identify Mushrooms?

  1. Observe the cap shape
  2. Check the gill color
  3. Note the habitat
  4. Smell the mushroom (some have distinct aromas)
  5. Check for a ring or volva at the base

Medicinal Uses of Mushrooms

Mushrooms have been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Some notable medicinal mushrooms include:

  • Reishi: Known for its immune-boosting and anti-aging properties.
  • Turkey Tail: Used in cancer research for its potential anti-tumor effects.
  • Lion's Mane: Helps improve cognitive function and nerve regeneration.
